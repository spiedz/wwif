Where the original "Den of Thieves" grounded itself in recognizable Los Angeles geography, "Pantera" uses its European settings to suggest that both predators and prey have evolved beyond their original hunting grounds, requiring new strategies and revealing hidden depths of character resilience and adaptability.

The warm, open landscapes of Tenerife symbolize the initial confidence and optimism of characters believing they can control their international expansion, while Belfast's industrial, weather-challenged environment reflects the harsh realities and unexpected complications that await them.

Production designer Terry Ackland-Snow faced the unique challenge of creating visual continuity between locations that exist in completely different climatic and architectural contexts. His solution involved developing a color palette that could translate across both environments, emphasizing steel blues and warm ambers that appear naturally in both Tenerife's golden hour lighting and Belfast's industrial atmosphere. This careful attention to visual cohesion helps audiences accept the geographic transitions without losing narrative momentum.

The casting of local talent proved crucial to establishing authentic atmosphere in both locations. In the Canary Islands, the production worked with Spanish casting directors to find actors who could deliver dialogue in multiple languages while maintaining the hard-edged criminal authenticity the franchise demands. Belfast's casting process focused on finding performers who could convincingly portray both criminal operatives and law enforcement personnel within the film's specific European context.
