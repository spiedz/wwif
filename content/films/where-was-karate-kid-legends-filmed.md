# Where Was Karate Kid: Legends (2025) Filmed? The Global Quest That United Two Masters

![Karate Kid: Legends filming locations](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1567002304689-56a3b28473c1?crop=entropy&cs=tinysrgb&fit=max&fm=jpg&ixid=M3w3NDI1MDZ8MHwxfHNlYXJjaHwxfHxNb250cmVhbCUyQyUyMFF1ZWJlYyUyQyUyMENhbmFkYXxlbnwwfDB8fHwxNzQ4MjY4MDEyfDA&ixlib=rb-4.1.0&q=80&w=1080)

For the first time in Karate Kid history, two iconic mentors share the screen as Ralph Macchio's Daniel LaRusso and Jackie Chan's Mr. Han unite across continents. *Karate Kid: Legends* doesn't just tell a story about martial arts‚Äîit embarks on a global journey that spans Montreal's urban landscapes, Tokyo's ancient dojos, and India's vibrant streets, creating the most internationally ambitious entry in the beloved franchise.

Director Jonathan Entwistle chose to film across eight distinct locations, each representing different martial arts philosophies and cultural approaches to discipline, respect, and personal growth. The result is a cinematic tapestry that honors the universal language of martial arts while showcasing the unique character of each filming destination.

## üåç The Story Behind the Global Journey

*Karate Kid: Legends* follows Li Fong (Ben Wang), a young martial artist who discovers that mastering karate requires understanding its connections to fighting disciplines worldwide. When Daniel LaRusso and Mr. Han join forces as mentors, their combined wisdom takes Li on a transformative journey from Montreal's bustling streets to Tokyo's traditional training halls, ultimately revealing that true martial arts mastery transcends cultural boundaries.

The film's international scope reflects a growing trend in modern cinema‚Äîauthentic location shooting that serves the story rather than simply providing exotic backdrops. Each filming location was chosen not just for visual appeal, but for its genuine connection to martial arts traditions and cultural authenticity.

This marks the sixth theatrical film in the Karate Kid franchise and the first to feature both Ralph Macchio and Jackie Chan sharing significant screen time, uniting the original trilogy's spirit with the modern reboot's international perspective.

### üá®üá¶ Montreal, Quebec - The Production Heart

![Montreal filming locations](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1567002304689-56a3b28473c1?crop=entropy&cs=tinysrgb&fit=max&fm=jpg&ixid=M3w3NDI1MDZ8MHwxfHNlYXJjaHwxfHxNb250cmVhbCUyQyUyMFF1ZWJlYyUyQyUyMENhbmFkYXxlbnwwfDB8fHwxNzQ4MjY4MDEyfDA&ixlib=rb-4.1.0&q=80&w=1080)

Montreal served as the film's primary production base, hosting 75% of principal photography across its remarkably diverse urban landscape. The city's unique blend of North American efficiency and European charm provided the perfect foundation for a story about cultural convergence in martial arts.

**Key Scenes Filmed:**
- Li Fong's introduction and early training montages in urban environments
- Daniel LaRusso's arrival and first meetings with local martial arts practitioners
- Pivotal mentor-student conversations between all three main characters
- Tournament preparation sequences in various Montreal neighborhoods

**Why Montreal Works:**
Quebec's generous film tax incentives made the ambitious international production financially viable, but Montreal's appeal extends far beyond economics. The city's multicultural neighborhoods allow single locations to represent multiple international settings, while its experienced film infrastructure supported complex martial arts choreography and international cast logistics.

**Production Insights:**
The Montreal shoot ran from April through June 2024, taking advantage of the city's spring transformation. Director Entwistle specifically chose this timing to capture Montreal's emergence from winter‚Äîa visual metaphor for Li Fong's personal growth throughout the story. Over 200 local crew members worked alongside international department heads to create seamless production workflows.

**Specific Montreal Filming Areas:**

**Chinatown District**
Montreal's vibrant Chinatown provided authentic Asian cultural atmosphere without requiring international travel for every scene. The area's traditional architecture, authentic restaurants, and cultural centers allowed filmmakers to maintain visual continuity with Tokyo-shot sequences while providing practical shooting convenience.

The famous "mentor meeting" scene, where Daniel and Mr. Han first acknowledge their shared commitment to Li's training, was filmed at the Jardin de la Pagode, with the traditional Chinese garden providing symbolic backdrop for their philosophical alignment.

**Old Port Waterfront**
The historic Old Port's cobblestone streets and 18th-century architecture provided dramatic backdrops for key emotional sequences. The area's unique blend of historic preservation and modern vitality perfectly captured the film's theme of honoring tradition while embracing change.

The climactic "understanding scene," where Li finally integrates his various martial arts lessons, was filmed along the waterfront promenade at sunrise, with the St. Lawrence River providing natural beauty that enhanced the character's moment of clarity.

**Mount Royal Park**
The city's iconic mountain park served as natural training grounds for outdoor martial arts sequences. The park's varied terrain‚Äîfrom gentle slopes to challenging hiking trails‚Äîallowed choreographers to design training sequences that emphasized different aspects of martial arts conditioning.

Early morning shoots captured the park's peaceful atmosphere, essential for scenes depicting meditation and mental discipline training. The summit's panoramic city views provided stunning visual metaphors for Li's expanding perspective on martial arts and life.

### üáØüáµ Tokyo, Japan - Honoring Ancient Traditions

![Tokyo martial arts filming](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1526481280693-3bfa7568e0f3?crop=entropy&cs=tinysrgb&fit=max&fm=jpg&ixid=M3w3NDI1MDZ8MHwxfHNlYXJjaHwxfHxUb2t5byUyQyUyMEphcGFufGVufDB8MHx8fDE3NDgyNjgwMTd8MA&ixlib=rb-4.1.0&q=80&w=1080)

Tokyo's sequences represent the film's spiritual and technical heart, showcasing authentic Japanese martial arts traditions in their natural environment. The production gained unprecedented access to historic dojos and training facilities, working directly with martial arts masters whose lineages trace back centuries.

**Key Sequences Filmed:**
- Traditional dojo training with authentic sensei and students
- Mr. Han's backstory sequences revealing his connection to Japanese martial arts
- Cultural exchange moments highlighting differences between Chinese and Japanese approaches
- Tournament sequences in authentic Japanese martial arts venues

**Cultural Authenticity:**
The Tokyo shoot required extensive cultural consultation to ensure respectful representation of Japanese martial arts traditions. Production worked with the All Japan Karatedo Federation and several traditional dojo masters to maintain authenticity in everything from bow protocols to training methodologies.

**Historic Dojo Access:**
Several scenes were filmed in the Shotokan Karate International headquarters, where the style featured in the original Karate Kid films originated. This location provided direct historical connection to the franchise's roots while showcasing the evolution of karate practice in its homeland.

**Modern Tokyo Integration:**
Contemporary Tokyo scenes were filmed in Shibuya, Harajuku, and Shinjuku districts, showing how traditional martial arts continue thriving in ultra-modern urban environments. These sequences emphasize that martial arts traditions adapt and survive by embracing rather than rejecting change.

**Production Challenges:**
Tokyo filming required careful coordination with local authorities and martial arts organizations. Traditional dojo protocols meant filming schedules had to accommodate regular classes and ceremonies, with production crews learning proper etiquette for working in sacred training spaces.

## üé≠ Behind-the-Scenes Production Stories

### The Historic First Meeting
Ralph Macchio and Jackie Chan had never worked together before *Karate Kid: Legends*, despite both being martial arts cinema icons. Their first scene together‚Äîfilmed in Montreal's Chinatown‚Äîrequired multiple takes not due to technical problems, but because both actors were genuinely starstruck by finally collaborating.

### International Casting Challenges
Ben Wang's casting as Li Fong required extensive martial arts training across multiple disciplines. The young actor spent six months in pre-production learning basics of karate, kung fu, and Kalaripayattu, working with masters from each tradition to develop believable competence in all three styles.

### Weather Adaptation
Montreal's unpredictable spring weather forced creative adaptations. When unexpected snow fell during outdoor training sequences in Mount Royal Park, director Entwistle incorporated the weather into the story, creating powerful visual metaphors about overcoming unexpected challenges.

### Cultural Protocol Navigation
Filming in traditional Japanese dojos required extensive cultural education for the entire crew. Production assistants learned proper bowing protocols, camera operators practiced moving respectfully through sacred spaces, and craft services adapted to dietary restrictions and timing requirements of traditional training schedules.

### Language Barrier Solutions
With cast and crew speaking English, French, Japanese, Mandarin, and Hindi, communication required creative solutions. The production employed multiple translators and developed visual communication systems for complex fight choreography that transcended language barriers.
