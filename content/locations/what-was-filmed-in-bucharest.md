## üì∏ Photography Tips for Film Location Spotting

Bucharest offers endless opportunities for stunning photos, especially when capturing its cinematic spirit.

- **Parliament Palace:** Wide-angle shots from Constitution Square, emphasizing its scale. Try a low angle to make it even more imposing.
- **Old Town:** Cobblestone streets, colorful buildings, and lively outdoor cafes. Capture candid moments of people enjoying the vibrant atmosphere. Early morning is great to avoid crowds.

- **Wide-angle lens:** Essential for capturing the grandeur of Parliament Palace and the Athenaeum.

- **Tripod:** For night shots of illuminated buildings or long exposures of traffic around Arcul de Triumf.
- **Drone (with caution/permits):** Offers unparalleled aerial views, especially of Parliament Palace (check local regulations and no-fly zones thoroughly).

### **Timing and Lighting Advice**
- **Golden Hour:** Sunrise and sunset provide the most beautiful, soft light, perfect for illuminating Bucharest's facades and creating dramatic contrasts.
- **Blue Hour:** Just after sunset, when the sky turns deep blue and city lights come on. Excellent for capturing illuminated landmarks.
- **Overcast days:** Can be great for uniform lighting, especially for detailed architectural shots, avoiding harsh shadows.

### **Respectful Photography Guidelines**
- **People:** Always be respectful when photographing people. Ask for permission if taking close-up portraits.
- **Private Property:** Be mindful of privacy, especially in residential areas or near private buildings.
- **Safety:** Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or when crossing intersections for a shot.
- **Drones:** Romania has strict drone regulations. Obtain necessary permits and be aware of no-fly zones (e.g., around Parliament Palace, airports). Always fly responsibly.
